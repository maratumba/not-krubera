No, That Is Not Krubera Cave

You are here probably because you saw this picture on the internet and it said it was the Krubera Cave, the deepest cave in the world. It is not. It's a picture of the Fantastic Pit in Ellison's Cave, which is in Georgia, US.

The pit (or the drop) is 179 meters (586 feet) deep and is the deepest pitch in continental US. Ellison's Cave is the 12th deepest cave in the US with a total depth of 324 meters (1063 feet).

Krubera - Voronya Cave is in Georgia and is the second deepest cave with 2199 meters (7215 feet) depth. The deepest cave in the world is Veryovkina Cave at 2223 meters (7293 feet).

No They Are Not The Closest Points To Earth's Center

Cave entrances of most of the caves are high up in the mountains so even the deepest point is not below sea level. So they are not "the closest point to the center of the earth" by a long shot.
